OPA Tool Login

The LetsGetChecked Onsite Provider Application (OPA) is the tablet-based platform you use to manage all appointments, check-ins, and data entry at events.

🔑
Your Login Credentials
Your username is your US Wellness field operations email: firstname.lastname@fo.uswellness.com
Example: shea.wallace@fo.uswellness.com
📧Step 1 — Create Your Account
  • 1
    Look for an account setup email from No-reply@appus.letsgetchecked.com — sent to your US Wellness email address on file.
  • 2
    Click the "Confirm email" button in the email to activate your account and set your password.
  • 3
    If you haven't received the email, check spam/junk folders before contacting US Wellness.
🌐Step 2 — Sign In (3-Step Process)

Go to https://onsite-provider.letsgetchecked.com/ and complete the following steps:

Step 1
Enter Email
Type your @fo.uswellness.com address and click Continue
→
Step 2
Enter Password
Enter your password and click Sign In
→
Step 3
Verify Identity
Enter the authentication code emailed to you, then click Verify your identity
⚠️
Identity Verification Required Every Login
The OPA uses two-factor authentication. You will receive a new code via email each time you sign in. Keep your email accessible during the event.
📅Step 3 — Locate Your Event
  • 1
    After signing in, you will land on the My Events page showing today's assigned events.
  • 2
    Select today's event to open the appointment list.
  • 3
    Multiple event types (e.g., Rapid Test, Flu Immunization) may appear — be sure to select the correct one.
📞
Event Not Showing?
If your event is not visible within 24 hours of start time, contact US Wellness immediately.

Appointment Management

Efficient appointment management is the foundation of fast event throughput. Understand the five appointment views and how to handle each participant type.

⚡
Target: 10-Minute Throughput
On-time appointments always take priority. Maintain a 10-minute per participant pace to keep the event on schedule.
🗂Appointment View Tabs

The OPA has five filter views. Each role should work from a specific view:

All
📋
Every appointment today, in chronological order
Walk In
🚶
Only same-day walk-in appointments
Scheduled
✅
Not yet checked in — Greeter preferred view
Checked In
🩺
Checked in, awaiting screening — Screener preferred view
Complete
🏁
Screening fully completed today
👤Handling Each Participant Type
On-Time
Walk-In
Not on List
  • 1
    Work from the Scheduled tab to see participants who haven't checked in yet.
  • 2
    When participant arrives, locate their name and click the "Check In" button next to it.
  • 3
    Appointment moves to the Checked In view, ready for the screener to begin data entry.
⭐
Priority Rule
On-time appointments ALWAYS take priority over walk-ins and late arrivals.
⚠️
Walk-Ins Must Be Registered First
Participants must register via UHC Rewards, Optum Engage, or the LetsGetChecked app before they can be scheduled as a walk-in. A walk-in slot is not a guarantee of screening — slots are filled as time and supplies allow.
  • 1
    Navigate to the Walk In tab to see same-day registrants.
  • 2
    Click "Schedule" next to the participant's name.
  • 3
    Select an available time slot — choose Now, an available slot, or Other to enter a specific time.
  • 4
    Participant is now scheduled. Proceed with check-in when their slot arrives.
  • 1
    Ask the participant to show their confirmation email.
  • 2
    Verify the date and location on their confirmation. If it matches your event, text their name and a photo of their confirmation email to USW for verification.
  • 3
    If they registered for a different date or location, ask them to show their confirmation email to verify they have an appointment. You will not be able to enter their results in the OPA today. Screen them using a paper form, write "NOT ENTERED" on the form, and place it on top of the stack when returning forms to US Wellness.
  • 4
    If the participant has no appointment at all, they must first register on UHC Rewards, Optum Engage, or the LGC app to create an appointment.
Section 4 of 6

OPA Data Entry

Accurate data entry is critical. Greeter staff complete the lifestyle questionnaire; screeners enter all biometric lab results.

🎥
Video Walkthrough — Watch Before Proceeding
Review the OPA data entry training video before your first event.
📋Greeter Role
  • 1
    Open participant record from Scheduled tab after check-in.
  • 2
    Verify participant name and DOB match the ID presented.
  • 3
    Complete the Lifestyle questionnaire with the participant.
  • 4
    Click "Save & next" to pass record to screener.
🩸Screener Role
  • 1
    Work from the Checked In tab to see participants ready for screening.
  • 2
    Confirm name and DOB again before entering results.
  • 3
    Enter ALL Biometric Lab Results: Glucose, Total Cholesterol, HDL, Triglycerides. LDL and Total/HDL ratio auto-calculate.
  • 4
    Check your work — review all values before saving.
  • 5
    Click "Save and Complete".
🕐Late Data Entry

Use this procedure when you cannot enter data in real time (e.g., no WiFi, system outage).

1
Go to the Lifestyle page for the participant
Find the record in the Checked In tab.
2
Toggle "Late Entry" ON at the top of the page
A yellow banner will appear confirming you are in late entry mode.
3
Select the provider / screener name
Choose the staff member who actually performed the screening.
4
Enter the time the test was completed
This is the actual screening time — NOT the time you are entering data now.
5
Enter reason for late entry
Options: No WiFi / System down / Other (add a note explaining)
6
Complete Lifestyle and Lab Results, then Save and Complete
⚠️Incomplete Results Entry

Use "Close without finishing" only as a last resort. Acceptable reasons:

  • 🚨Emergency treatment required for the participant
  • 🚶Participant leaves the event or refuses to continue
  • 🧪Inability to collect the sample
🚫
Do Not Use Casually
"Close without finishing" should be used only when absolutely necessary. It leaves the record incomplete and impacts participant data.

Private Result Review

A brief, private results consultation must be completed with every participant before they leave the screening area.

📝
Fill In ALL Results Fields
Complete every field in the OPA — including auto-calculated values like BMI and LDL. The participant takes home the yellow copy; you retain the white copy and return it to USW.
🩺Result Review Process
  • 1
    Turn the hard copy Onsite Event Form to the back side, which shows reference ranges for each metric.
  • 2
    In-Range Metrics: Tell the participant which values fall within normal/optimal ranges. Encourage them to discuss with their healthcare provider how to maintain those results.
  • 3
    Out-of-Range Metrics: Identify which values are outside normal ranges and recommend speaking with their healthcare provider about steps to improve those results.
  • 4
    Provide the yellow (bottom) copy of the Onsite Event Form to the participant and encourage them to share results with their doctor.
  • 5
    Retain the white (top) copy and return it to US Wellness at the end of the event.
📊Key Metrics Reviewed
  • 🩸Glucose (mg/dL)
  • 💉Total Cholesterol (mg/dL)
  • 💉HDL Cholesterol (mg/dL)
  • 💉LDL Cholesterol (auto-calc)
  • 💉Triglycerides (mg/dL)
  • ⚖️BMI (auto-calc)
  • 🫀Blood Pressure
🔒Privacy During Review
  • 🛡️Conduct review inside the screened area — no other participants should be able to hear
  • 🗣️Speak in a low, calm voice
  • 📋Show the participant their own form only — do not reference others' results
  • ⚕️Direct all clinical questions to their healthcare provider — do not diagnose or advise treatment

Specimen Labeling & Shipping

Professional handling of venous tubes ensures accurate results. Proper labeling and packaging are required for every specimen.

🔬Scanning & Labeling Tubes
  • 1
    Scan the Alpha Code and Numerical Code into the patient record on the tablet. Codes are case sensitive and require dashes.
    • Numerical Code format: LGC-1234-5678-9012
    • Alpha Code format: ABCDEF
  • 2
    Complete the biowaste bag label with:
    • Date of birth
    • Sex (as shown in appointment record)
    • Sample collection date and time
  • 3
    Match codes on tubes to codes on the biowaste bag label — verify before sealing.
  • 4
    Place both tubes into the biowaste bag and seal the bag completely.
⚠️
Scanning is Required — Codes are Case Sensitive
Always scan rather than manually type codes. The Alpha Code and Numerical Code are case sensitive and require dashes exactly as printed.
🚚Shipping
  • 1
    Place the sealed biowaste bag(s) inside the white U-LINE shipping box and tape the box closed.
  • 2
    Affix the UPS label provided to the outside of the sealed white shipping box.
  • 3
    Confirm the label is UPS NEXT DAY AIR to MONROVIA, CA before dropping off.
✅
Double-Check Before Shipping
Verify the shipping label destination (Monrovia, CA) and service level (UPS Next Day Air) before handing off the package.

Pillars of Success

Every LetsGetChecked onsite event is built on four pillars. These standards define the participant experience and your professional conduct.

⭐Essential Must Knows
  • 1
    Participant Satisfaction Survey QR Code flyers should be posted at each station so participants can scan and submit after their screening. You also received one Site Coordinator QR Code flyer, which should be given to the site coordinator upon arrival.
  • 2
    Consent forms are signed digitally — you do not need a paper copy unless you have been given approval to screen a participant who was unable to register in the OPA.
  • 3
    Post-event form count: After your event has ended, you will receive a link via text. Click the link to submit the number of total forms. Verify this matches the number marked "Completed" in the OPA. If the counts do not match, call 1-833-363-1394 to report any missing member(s).
⚡FAST — Throughput
  • ✅On-time appointments always get priority
  • ✅Late and walk-in slots filled as available
  • ✅Maintain 10-minute per-participant pace
  • ✅Greeter and screener work their preferred tabs simultaneously
